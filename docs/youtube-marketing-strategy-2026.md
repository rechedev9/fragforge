# YouTube Marketing Strategy for ZackVideo CS2

Updated: 2026-06-02

## Core Thesis

ZackVideo should use Shorts as the discovery engine and long videos as the loyalty engine.

For this channel, the strongest content pillars are:

1. Cheater POV revenge reels: every time we kill the suspected cheater, show killer POV, then target POV dying.
2. Pro/player all-kill compilations: one long vertical Short per player/game, not separate one-kill Shorts.
3. Utility Shorts: clean smoke/flash/molotov lineups with accurate labels.
4. Long-form match stories: 8-12 minute narrative edits from the best matches, especially cheating, comeback, clutch, or high-ELO themes.

The channel should optimize for viewer response, not algorithm superstition. YouTube says recommendations are driven by personalization plus video performance: whether viewers choose to watch, keep watching, engage positively, and seem satisfied. For Shorts, YouTube specifically names `% of viewers who chose to view`, average view duration, average percentage viewed, likes/dislikes, and survey satisfaction as ranking signals. For long videos, it names click behavior, average view duration, average percentage viewed, and satisfaction.

New refinement from the second research pass:

- Long Shorts are now structurally viable. YouTube classifies square or vertical uploads up to 3 minutes as Shorts, so the channel rule should stay: one longer Short with the full kill story, not one Short per kill.
- Every good Short should have a path to more watch time: related video when a matching long video exists, pinned comment, playlist, or a follow-up upload.
- Thumbnail strategy must be split by format. Long videos get real custom thumbnail work and Test & Compare. Shorts get a selected in-video frame and a strong first second.
- Cheater content should stay funny, gameplay-focused, and evidence-based. Avoid personal harassment, doxxing, brigading, or calls for abuse.

## Shorts Strategy

Shorts are for fast discovery, replayable jokes, and emotional hooks. A ZackVideo Short should start immediately with the payoff context. No long intro.

Recommended formats:

- Cheater reel: `we kill him -> his POV dying`, repeated for every death.
- Pro all-kills: all kills from one player in one match, in order.
- Utility: one useful lineup or one compact map pack.
- Funny fail: one story, one punchline, no explanation card unless necessary.

Length rules:

- 15-35 seconds: fast joke, single clutch, single utility lineup.
- 45-120 seconds: default range for cheater death reels and pro/player all-kill compilations.
- 120-180 seconds: allowed when the story still moves quickly, especially "killer POV -> target POV dying" sequences.
- Over 180 seconds: turn it into a long-form story and publish the best 45-90 seconds as a Short that points to the full video.

For Shorts longer than 60 seconds, avoid third-party claimed music. YouTube says Shorts over one minute with an active Content ID claim can be blocked globally.

Targets to monitor in YouTube Studio:

- `How many chose to view`: if weak, the first frame/title is not strong enough.
- Average view duration and average percentage viewed: if weak, cut dead time and make the first 2 seconds more obvious.
- Likes, comments, shares, subscribers gained: if strong, keep the format as a repeatable series.
- For Shorts, use "Engaged views" in Analytics to compare performance, because YouTube changed public Shorts view counting in 2025.

Production rules:

- Preserve real gameplay/HUD for credibility.
- Keep final Shorts vertical 1080x1920, H.264/AAC, 60fps.
- All-kill/player requests become one long vertical Short by default.
- Cheater reels must pass block-by-block QA before upload.
- Use a selected frame as the Shorts thumbnail. Pick a frame with visible enemy, killfeed, scoreboard, or POV context. Do not spend production time on custom thumbnail variants for Shorts.

## Long Video Strategy

Long videos should not be raw dumps. They should be story edits with a clear promise:

- "We played against a suspected cheater and still won."
- "The most blatant Dust2 game we have played."
- "How we beat a 25k ELO suspected cheater."

Recommended structure:

1. 0:00-0:08: cold open with the funniest or most suspicious moment.
2. 0:08-0:25: one-sentence premise, no long channel intro.
3. 0:25-70%: escalating rounds, suspicious moments, key kills.
4. Last 30%: comeback, win condition, best revenge deaths.
5. Final 10 seconds: quick CTA to watch the Shorts version or another cheater video.

Long-video metrics:

- CTR by traffic source: compare Search vs Home/Suggested separately.
- 30-second retention: YouTube notes that 50%+ after 30 seconds is above typical for intros.
- Average view duration: long videos need absolute watch time, not only percentage watched.
- Returning viewers: long videos should build regular viewers, not just one-off Shorts traffic.

Thumbnail and title testing:

- Build 2-3 thumbnail variants before publishing important long videos.
- Use actual gameplay evidence, not generic blurred backgrounds: enemy POV, scoreboard, killfeed, or the funniest suspicious moment.
- Keep thumbnail text to 2-4 words: `WE WON`, `CHEATER POV`, `25K PREMIER`, `EVERY DEATH`.
- Use YouTube Test & Compare for public long-form videos when available. It tests up to three thumbnails and evaluates by watch-time share, not just click-through rate.
- Do not wait forever for a test result. YouTube says winners can take days or up to 2 weeks, and sometimes there is no clear winner.

## Titles

Use English titles for global CS2 reach. Keep Spanish flavor in pinned comments, descriptions, or occasional title variants when the joke is better in Spanish.

Rules:

- Put `CS2` at the start or near the start.
- Front-load the hook before platform truncation.
- Avoid titles over 100 characters. For Shorts, YouTube's upload UI title limit is 100 characters.
- Avoid fake certainty if we cannot prove cheating. Use "suspected cheater" in safer uploads; use "cheater" only when the user intentionally wants that tone.
- Avoid all-caps spam. One emphasized word is fine; a title screaming every word is not.
- Mentions are allowed in titles and descriptions. If crediting another YouTube channel, type `@` and choose the channel from YouTube's suggested list. Use mentions for credit/context, not as a ranking trick.

## Hashtags

Use 3 hashtags in the description, not a wall of tags. YouTube displays up to three description hashtags next to the title, and excessive hashtags reduce relevance. YouTube ignores all hashtags if a video has more than 60, and hashtags must not be misleading or used for harassment.

Default sets:

- General CS2: `#CS2 #CounterStrike2 #CS2Highlights`
- Premier/cheater: `#CS2 #CounterStrike2 #CS2Premier`
- Pro event: `#CS2 #CounterStrike2 #IEMCologne`
- Utility: `#CS2 #CounterStrike2 #CS2Lineups`
- Map-specific: replace the third tag with `#Dust2`, `#Mirage`, `#Inferno`, `#Nuke`, etc.

Avoid using both broad and low-value tags just to fill space. `#Shorts` is optional; use it only if it does not replace a more relevant tag.

Tags are different from hashtags. Tags in Studio can help with misspellings like `Counter Strike`, `CounterStrike`, `free sil3a`, or player names, but YouTube says title, thumbnail, and description are more important and tags otherwise play a minimal discovery role.

## Research Workflow Before Upload

Before writing the final title for an important upload:

1. Check YouTube Studio Analytics > Trends for:
   - `CS2 cheater`
   - `CS2 Premier cheater`
   - `CS2 Dust2`
   - `CS2 all kills`
   - player/map terms such as `NAF Nuke`, `Boombl4 Dust2`, `IEM Cologne CS2`
2. Save useful search terms and note content gaps.
3. Check the Inspiration tab for title/thumbnail angles, but treat AI suggestions as drafts only.
4. Pick one primary query, one emotional hook, and one proof detail:
   - Query: `CS2 Premier cheater`
   - Hook: `we still won`
   - Proof detail: `killer POV then his POV dying`
5. Use that trio consistently in title, first description line, pinned comment, and playlist.

## Shorts-To-Long Funnel

Use Shorts to create the first spike, then point interested viewers to the deeper story.

Rules:

- If a long video exists, set it as the Short's related video in YouTube Studio.
- If the long video is not ready yet, pin a comment promising the full version and replace it when published.
- Use one shared phrase across both uploads so viewers understand the connection:
  - Short: `CS2 | We Beat a Suspected Cheater and Showed Every Death POV`
  - Long: `CS2 | The Full Match Where We Beat a Suspected Cheater`
- Put both in `CS2 vs Cheaters` playlist immediately after upload.
- Reply to the best early comment with the long-video link once the long video is public.

Source: https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/14075157

## Cheater Video Safety

Cheater videos are funny because of the gameplay reversal, not because the channel sends viewers after a person.

Rules:

- Default wording is `suspected cheater` unless the user intentionally wants a more aggressive title.
- Do not show private information outside normal public game/demo identifiers.
- Do not ask viewers to harass, raid, mass-report, or contact the player.
- Keep pinned comments as prompts about the match, not the person:
  - `Which death was the funniest?`
  - `Would you have called this suspicious?`
  - `Was the killer POV -> his POV format clear?`
- If including another creator with `@handle`, use it as credit/context only.

Source: https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802268

## Upload Times From Spain

YouTube says publish time is not known to affect long-term performance, but uploading when the audience is active can help immediate views. The real source of truth is YouTube Studio > Analytics > Audience > `When your viewers are on YouTube`, which shows when viewers were online in the last 28 days.

Sources:

- Audience report: https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/9314416
- Performance FAQ on publish time: https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/141805

## Cadence

YouTube says there is no required posting cadence and Shorts do not hurt long-form recommendations. So the schedule should be sustainable:

- 3 Shorts per week minimum when there is demo material.
- 5 Shorts per week during event/pro-demo bursts.
- 1 long video every 1-2 weeks.
- 1 Community post or poll on weeks without a long video.

Weekly example:

| Day | Upload |
| --- | --- |
| Tuesday 20:00 | Short: pro all-kills or utility |
| Thursday 20:00 | Short: funny/cheater/clip |
| Friday 18:30 | Main Short: best clip of week |
| Sunday 10:30 | Long video every 1-2 weeks |
| Sunday 20:00 | Short recap if no long video |

## 48-Hour Review Loop

Check every upload after 2 hours, 24 hours, and 48 hours.

For Shorts:

- Low `chose to view`: improve first frame/title.
- Good `chose to view`, low retention: cut earlier, reduce dead time, show payoff sooner.
- Good retention, low engagement: improve ending, pinned comment, or story clarity.
- Strong comments: reply fast and pin the funniest/most useful comment.

For long videos:

- Low CTR vs channel baseline: change title/thumbnail, but only if impressions are meaningful.
- Drop in first 30 seconds: cold open is too slow or title promised a different story.
- Strong retention but low impressions: topic may be niche; make Shorts that funnel into it.

Do not delete/reupload casually. Change title/thumbnail/description when evidence points to a presentation problem.
